Judge - morality policing. Unlike the Mastermind, the Inquisitor believes their control to be just.

Discipline classes tend to be susceptible to attacks from other angles, but this is where the Inquisitor steps in to root out trickery. Armed with a cool head and honed training, this class excels at combining debuffs and defenses to corner and quash an opponent's assault. An Inquisitor's bread and butter revolves around exposing an enemy's strategy with their high [[Cunning]], and blunting its effectiveness with their high [[Discipline]]. As opposed to an Assassin, who respond perfectly to a single move, Inquisitors gradually defang their opponent.

The Inquisitor's passive, Veritas, gradually reveals the enemy's entire gameplan.

UNIQUE:
Veritas (P): Each turn you combat with an enemy, declare a skill name. The enemy exposes that skill if they possess it
Shackle: All enemies lose a combat stat based on the number of their exposed skills
Trial: Target an enemy and declare an action type/combat stat (T3: enemy uses ACT type). An ally restores HP/STA, and the enemy's combat stat is reduced permanently

DISCIPLINE:
Neutralize (T2: target enemy attacks): Block
Truestrike: Unaffected by locks and debuffs. Small strike
Disarm: Attack. Choose whether enemy loses ATK or DEF
[[Traits/Discipline#Chain Mail|Chain Mail]]: Block. Lock 1 enemy attacks that are fully blocked this turn

CUNNING:
[[Cunning#Profile|Profile]]: Expose all skills of one action type in enemy skillset

[[Cunning#Coerce|Coerce]]: Target an enemy and declare an action type and an ACT slot. If that slot doesn't have that type next turn, they lose HP/STA
