A jack-of-all-trades who always has an ace up their sleeve, someone both [[Balance|Balanced]] and [[Cunning]]. Where most schemers must approach situations with preparations complete, Rogues formulate and discard plans on the fly.

Rogues gamble their influence against their enemies, making cold reads and stabs in the dark. With their quick thinking and agility, however, the game is stacked in their favor. A good Rogue can draw enemies into an enticing exchange, then constantly run interference and skirt the rules until they’ve won everything.

A Rogue excels at pulling the floor out from under enemies. Once they've exposed enough enemy skills, they can use Sleight of Hand to replace key skills with Bearings, clogging up their skillset.

UNIQUE:
Sleight of Hand: Move an enemy's exposed skill in skillset into memory, then add Bearings to their skillset (when added to skillset, opponent chooses an exposed skill to lose 1 use)
Pickpocket: Choose a random or exposed skill in the enemy's memory. They forget it, then if it was revealed, add it to your skillset
Thieves' Tools (P): After all skills resolve, if you exposed an enemy skill this turn, choose whether to reduce that enemy's Stealth, break one of your Locks, or Cycle 1

CUNNING:
Sneak Attack: Target an enemy and declare 3 ACT types. If this doesn't match any of them, big strike
Maneuver: Choose before committing - fully block the next attack this turn, or gain some block for each attack this turn
Glimpse: Target an enemy. They reveal their first ACT slot in 2 turns
Profile: Expose all skills of one action type in enemy skillset

BALANCE:
Circle: Block. If I'm added to memory, return me to the skillset
